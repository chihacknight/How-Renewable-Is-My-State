This visualization uses energy consumption data from the State Energy Data System (SEDS) pages of the US Energy Information Administrationâ€™s (EIA) website. All of the energy values contained in this visualization represent energy consumed in billions of BTU. A more detailed overview of how we arrived at these values is outlined below.

Methodology

All of the energy consumption categories contained in this visualization rely on the definitions used by the EIA:

  • Total Energy Consumption
  • Fossil Fuels - fossil fuels total consumption
    • Coal - coal total consumption
    • Natural Gas - natural gas total consumption (excluding supplemental gaseous fuels)
    • Petroleum - all petroleum products total consumption excluding fuel ethanol
  • Nuclear - nuclear energy consumed for electricity generation, total
  • Renewable Energy - renewable energy total consumption
    • Fuel Ethanol - fuel ethanol, excluding denaturant, total consumption
    • Geothermal - geothermal energy, total consumed
    • Hydropower - hydropower, total consumed
    • Solar - photovoltaic and solar thermal energy, total consumed
    • Wood and Waste - wood and waste total consumed
    • Wind - wind energy, total consumed
  • Interstate Imports/Exports - net interstate flow of electricity and associated losses (negative and positive values).
